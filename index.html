About

Humans of Hispaniola is an organization created to help tell the story of the men, women, and children living on the island of Hispaniola. Sharing an island just southeast of the United States, Haiti and the Dominican Republic have a long history of clash, and difficulties persist today. Our goal is to give a glimpse into the reality of life in Hispaniola through the faces and stories of the people weâ€™ve encountered.

Our mission at Humans of Hispaniola is to make these voices heard. Our mission is one of solidarity and subsidiarity. Humans of Hispaniola aims to display how we are connected to the people of Hispaniola, and to share their stories in the most direct way possible. The photos you see were taken on our trip in January 2017. The stories you hear are not told by us. They are told by the people you see.

Roots

We are an organization founded by a group of Boston College students as part of the Pedro Arrupe International Immersion program. The Arrupe program enables students learn about the complex reality, from a historical, social, economic, political and religious perspective, of people from another country who struggle in poverty. The program sends 9 student groups to learn about and combat social injustices in impoverished countries. On our immersion trip, we were able to meet, speak, sing, dance, and accompany so many incredible people. Now our mission is to make their voices heard.
