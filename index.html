About

Humans of Hispaniola is an organization created to help tell the story of those living on the island of Hispaniola. Sharing an island just southeast of the United States, Haiti and the Dominican Republic have a long history of clash, and difficulties

persist today. Our goal is to give a glimpse into the reality of this life through the faces of the people we’ve encountered. Our mission is to make these voices heard. It is one of solidarity and subsidiarity. We aim to display how we are connected to the people of Hispaniola, and to share their stories in the most direct way possible. The photos you see were taken on our trip in

January 2017. The stories you hear are not told by us. We invite you to hear the stories just as we did. We invite you to grapple with the complex realities of statelessness, of political refugees, of poverty. We hope you take time to explore our website, explore the ways to get involved and to understand that this project is not the end. This is the beginning.

Roots

We are an organization founded by a group of Boston College students as part of the Pedro Arrupe International Immersion program. On our immersion trip, we were able to meet, speak, sing, dance, and accompany so many incredible people. Now our mission is to make their voices heard.

The Issues

Our trip opened our eyes to many of the injustices these people suffer on a regular basis. From corruption in leadership, denial of basic rights, machismo cultures, environmental degradation, and economic stagnation, the issues are vast.
