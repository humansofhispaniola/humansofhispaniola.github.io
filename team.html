Arrupe Dominican Republic/Haiti 2017

Boston College Pedro Arrupe International Immersion Program

The Arrupe program enables students learn about the complex reality, from a historical, social, economic, political and religious perspective, of people from another country who struggle in poverty. The program sends 9 student groups to learn about and combat social injustices in impoverished countries in South America and the Carribean. Our group was made up of 16 members, including students, student leads, and mentors.

For 8 months prior to the trip, we met weekly for 2 hours to learn about the history and issues facing Haiti and the Dominican Republic. In addition to our preparation and fundraising efforts, we took time to reflect on our own lifestyles and mentally prepare for the immersion.

In January 2017, we landed in Santo Domingo where we spent a few days being hosted by Jesuits at Manressa Loyola before bussing west to Jimani where SJM opened their quarters to us for the majority of our trip. We travelled to and across the Haiti-DR border to visit with more SJM representatives in Haiti, before spending another 2 days in the Bateys of sugar cane plantations. In each spot, we met incredible people, heard incredible stories, and walked with our new friends.
